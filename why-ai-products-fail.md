# Why Most AI Products Fail

Most AI products don’t fail because of bad models.

They fail because of bad product decisions.

---

## Failure Pattern 1: AI Without a Workflow

AI is added as a standalone feature.

But users don’t need intelligence in isolation.

They need it embedded in what they already do.

---

## Failure Pattern 2: No Reliability Layer

LLMs are probabilistic.

But products are expected to be reliable.

If you don’t design for:
- validation
- constraints
- fallback logic  

Your product breaks silently.

---

## Failure Pattern 3: Demo vs Reality

AI demos look impressive.

Production systems expose:
- latency  
- cost  
- edge cases  

Most teams design for demos, not usage.

---

## The Shift

Winning teams treat AI as:

→ a system to design  
→ not a feature to add  

---

## Takeaway

AI products fail when:
- intelligence is prioritized over usability  
- capability is prioritized over reliability  

The best products do the opposite.
