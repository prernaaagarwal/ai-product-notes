# AI Product Thinking

Writing about AI product decisions from a B2B SaaS PM perspective.

These essays come from active learning: reading, building small tools with AI, and applying an AI product lens to 9+ years of product experience across B2B SaaS, CRM/CX, and CCaaS. The goal is to develop a sharper framework for where AI genuinely improves products and where it doesn't.

**Not covered here:** Model benchmarks, hype cycles, or tool lists.  
**Covered here:** Product decisions, failure patterns, system design thinking, and practical tradeoffs.

## Core Beliefs

- AI is only useful when embedded in real workflows
- Reliability matters more than raw capability
- Prompts are systems, not strings
- The best AI products reduce user effort — they don't just add novelty
- Senior PM judgment is knowing when *not* to use AI
